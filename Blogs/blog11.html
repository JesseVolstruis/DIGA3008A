Programming justice:

Justice and ethics are deeply ingrained in both artistic and programming practices. This is even more true in a digital age. Digital architectures and platforms are not neutral. They can reinforce already existing hierarchies. As designers, we and the algorithms/systems we make can impact others greatly. Recognizing this and the responsibility we have is a crucial part of analysing my own coding and artistic practices when looking into the ethics surrounding it. The stuff I create can easily affect others. I must always be conscious about how I create.

In terms of how I would actually approach my programming and designing, I would try to apply the standards I've been taught in this course so far. Both this course and analysing a website's UI/UX ethics has taught me various principles that I could apply to my own design. One way would be prioritizing accessibility. An example would be designing a robust mobile layout for a website for those without PCs or laptops or ensuring screen reader compatibility. Another way is paying close attention to transparency. Ensuring honesty and user trust is important as a designer. I would try to avoid systems that exploit user data. Another is examining whether my design is based in any privilege.

Programmingor designing with ethics in min is an ongoing process. I probably made many mistakes in this regard when just starting out and will probably continue to not be perfect. The most I can do is be constantly conscious of ethics and how I design with it in mind.
