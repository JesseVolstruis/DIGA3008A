Website analysis:

Wits:

Pros:

-Fairly fast load times

-Navigation is simple: It's clear what page you're on as the title of the page is near the header and the path is displayed so that you can see exactly what sections you're in and how you got there which could mitigate confusion. The two navigation sections are present on every page so you can navigate to them from anywhere

-Searching for degrees is user-friendly: Directly underneath the banner is an option to search for degrees or diplomas. An alternative link for this is directly next to the logo on the home page making searching For degrees easier. If you search by keyword, the options are laid out neatly with each having a card with its relevant information such as how many years and a description. A degree's page lists relevant information and has a button to apply for it. The “study at wits” page also lists the application options.

-Student related information is easily accessible: The first two links next to the logo (or first two in the mobile menu) are related to students/studying at wits which is one of the more relevant things for a university site.

Cons:

-Lots of navigation options: The home page has 2 navigation sections in its header. (On mobile the menu has two navigation sections) Some sections have their own subsections. Can become confusing to navigate

-Home page can feel cluttered: The banner takes up a large amount of space on a laptop screen. (although it's decently sized on Mobile devices) The content between the degree search and virtual tour takes up a lot of space despite being more relevant in the news tab.

-Intrusive bot chat prompt: On most pages there is a prompt in the bottom corner for a chat bot message. This is intrusive and has no option for removal. (at least not an obvious one) There is very little information offered up front about this chat bot and so it can be confusing as to whether it's even relevant at all.

-Student tab's information hierarchy for mobile Despite the first link being to wits' main learning management system, (Ulwazi) the news sections are displayed before all the links regarding study information. On desktop the information is laid out concisely but on mobile the news section takes up a lot of space and the second Ulwazi link is pasted at the bottom instead of on the side.
