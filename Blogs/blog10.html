An ethical internet:

Much of what I think about an ethical internet relates back to previous blogs, specifically about the digital divide and colonialism in my code. For an ethical internet to exist, the inequalities and divides in various communities must be greatly considered. Otherwise, we would only be further feeding into the systems and hierarchies that create those divides. It also relates to decolonialism. A truly ethical internet would decentre the current dominant voices/perspectives, such as western, male, and corporate perspectives and make more room for groups/communities with less dominant voices in the online space.

An ethical internet would require that developers recognize their responsibility and impact they have as designers. This means developing digital infrastructure that minimizes harm, maximizes privacy, and protects users. These means analysing our code and how it affects others. It means prioritizing these considerations over our own conveniences.

With all these aspects I think are required for an ethical internet, it seems almost utopian or improbable. With the requirements being so demanding: decolonial design, transparency, design justice, it can feel like fighting an uphill battle. Powerful companies are dominant on the internet and their goals don't usually align with the ethics needed to make an ethical internet. The scale and complexity of the internet makes meeting these demands tough. With all of this in mind, an ethical internet seems less like a fully achievable goal and more like an ongoing process, where reaching the destination might not be fully possible.
